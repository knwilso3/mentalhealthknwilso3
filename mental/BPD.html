    What is Borderline-Personality-Disorder?

    Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD) is a mental health condition characterized by intense mood swings,
    unstable relationships, intense anger, and impulsive behavior.
    It causes significant difficulties in emotion regulation, a distorted self-image, and a fear of abandonment.

    How does someone develop BPD?

    Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD) typically develops from a complex interaction between genetic predisposition
    and adverse childhood experiences.

    How can I treat my Borderline-Personality-Disorder?

    Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD) is primarily treated with evidence-based psychotherapy,
    with Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) being the most recognized.
    Treatments focus on improving emotional regulation, reducing impulsivity, and enhancing interpersonal skills.
    Long-term outcomes are positive, with many individuals recovering over time.
