    • I never thought I would have a healthy relationship with food—or with myself. For years, my eating disorder controlled everything: what I ate, how I felt, how I saw my body, and even how I connected with other people. It convinced me that I wasn’t good enough unless I followed its rules. But those rules only led me deeper into isolation, exhaustion, and pain. Recovery didn’t happen overnight. In fact, it was one of the hardest things I’ve ever done. It meant facing fears I had avoided for so long—eating foods I labeled “off-limits,” letting go of control, and challenging the voice in my head that told me I wasn’t worthy. There were setbacks, days when I wanted to give up, and moments when progress felt impossible. But little by little, things began to change. With support, patience, and a willingness to keep going, I started to rebuild my life. I learned to listen to my body instead of fighting it. I discovered that food is not the enemy—it’s nourishment, culture, connection, and even joy. Most importantly, I began to see myself as more than my body or my struggles. Today, I’m not perfect, but I’m free in ways I never thought possible. I can enjoy meals without guilt, spend time with loved ones without anxiety, and pursue my passions with energy and clarity. Recovery gave me my life back. If you’re struggling, please know this: change is possible. You are not alone, and you are worthy of healing just as you are.
